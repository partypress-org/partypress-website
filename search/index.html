FAQ

I found a bug or bad data. Where do I report it?

Please open an issue at GitHub Issues (partypress_website). That includes problems with the search site, the data, or the API. When reporting a problem with a specific press release, please include if you can: the URL of the release, the ParlGov party ID, and the date of the release (as shown in the table). That helps us locate and fix the record quickly.

I want to donate party press release data. How?

We welcome contributions. Please provide data as CSV with these columns (dates in YYYY-MM-DD format):

  • date — release date
  • date_collected — when you scraped/collected the row
  • country
  • party
  • title
  • text
  • url (optional but recommended)
  • parlgov_party_id (optional; ParlGov party ID)

Example:

date,date_collected,country,party,title,text,url,parlgov_party_id
2024-03-15,2024-03-20,Germany,SPD,Statement on climate law,"Full text of the press release…",https://example.org/press/123,523

Your submission should be accompanied by a waiver stating that you do not require attribution. You can use this text:

I release this data for use in PARTYPRESS without requiring attribution.

We will still try to credit contributors where we can. You can submit donations with waiver via the Issues page.

How often is the data updated?

Scraping is not done on a fixed schedule. We may update the corpus roughly yearly, but we do not promise any particular update frequency.

Are the data complete?

We take great care with data quality and consistency, but we cannot guarantee completeness or that every party press release is captured.

Do you have data for [country/party] X?

We publish all the data we have. The map and filters on this page show which countries and parties are in the corpus. If a country or party is missing, we do not have it (yet)—you can offer to donate data via the Issues page.

How can I cite PARTYPRESS?

A PARTYPRESS v2 citation will be made available here.

For PARTYPRESS v1, cite the database article: Erfort, C., Stoetzer, L. F., & Klüver, H. (2023). The PARTYPRESS Database. Research and Politics. https://doi.org/10.1177/20531680231183512. BibTeX is available on the partypress.org homepage (PARTYPRESS v1 section).

What is the license for the data?

Data are released under Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International (CC BY 4.0).

What are the CAP issue codes?

The issue labels (e.g. Environment, Health, Immigration) come from the Comparative Agendas Project (CAP). We adapted the CAP topic codebook to classify party press releases; the PARTYPRESS v1 article describes the methodology.
